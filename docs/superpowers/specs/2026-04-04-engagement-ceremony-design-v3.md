## Overview

The engagement ceremony establishes a secure messaging channel between two users' Alice (air-gapped) and Bob (networked) device pairs. The ceremony bootstraps trust, exchanges cryptographic key material, and enforces mandatory verification before any secure messaging occurs.

## Conventions

Throughout this specification, **"identity public key" used for ordering refers exclusively to the X25519 identity public key.** This applies to simultaneous initiation tiebreakers, safety number derivation, and any future operation requiring deterministic ordering. The Ed25519 signing public key is never used for ordering.

---

## 1. Threat Model Assumptions

1. **Alice is permanently air-gapped.** No network, no SIM, airplane mode enforced. All communication with Bob is QR-only.
2. **Bob-to-Bob internet channel is untrusted.** An adversary may intercept, modify, or replay payloads between the two Bobs. This is the primary MITM attack surface.
3. **The verification channel is independent.** Safety number verification happens over a voice/video call assumed to be harder to compromise than the Bob-to-Bob channel. **If the verification channel is also compromised, the ceremony cannot protect the user.**
4. **No physical meeting.** Users never meet in person. QR-based verification is not available.
5. **Paranoid posture.** Any anomaly — failed verification, interrupted ceremony, expired quarantine, new device — results in hard deletion of all associated material and a full restart. No partial trust. No shortcuts. No recovery from ambiguous state.

---

## 2. Participants and Roles

| Entity | Role | Capabilities |
|---|---|---|
| **Muttley's Alice** | Air-gapped crypto vault | Generates keys, encrypts, decrypts, stores contacts. Never touches network. |
| **Muttley's Bob** | Online courier | Relays key material via deep links (SMS/WhatsApp/Telegram). Never sees plaintext. |
| **Dastardly's Bob** | Online courier | Receives invitation, relays to Dastardly's Alice via QR. |
| **Dastardly's Alice** | Air-gapped crypto vault | Same as Muttley's Alice. |

**Initiator:** Always Bob. Only Bob can start an engagement — he has access to the Android contacts content provider and the internet transport.

### Simultaneous Initiation

If both Bobs send invitations to each other concurrently, the receiving Bob detects the collision. Deterministic tiebreaker: compare X25519 identity public keys lexicographically, lower wins (see Conventions). The losing invitation is silently discarded. Losing Bob displays: "Engagement already in progress — initiated by [contact]."

### Pending Invitation Cap

Bob holds a maximum of 7 pending inbound invitations. Beyond that, new invitations are silently dropped. **Known limitation:** the sender's Bob receives no feedback when an invitation is silently dropped. Muttley may believe he initiated engagement while Dastardly never sees it. A delivery receipt mechanism is out of scope (the Bob-to-Bob channel is untrusted), so silence does not imply acceptance. Users should confirm engagement receipt out-of-band if there is any doubt.

## 3. Ceremony Walkthrough

### Initiator Side (Muttley)

1. Muttley opens Bob.
2. Bob displays contacts from the Android content provider (display name + phone number).
3. Muttley selects Dastardly and taps the done icon.
4. Bob displays QR containing Dastardly's **phone number, display name, and a 16-byte ceremony nonce** generated by Bob. Alice stores this nonce with the synthetic contact so the final incoming bundle can be cryptographically bound to the original invitation. This prevents confusion if Muttley starts multiple ceremonies in rapid succession.
5. Muttley scans QR with Alice.
6. Alice ingests Dastardly's details and ceremony nonce, generates a synthetic contact ID, persists the contact.
7. Alice generates a key bundle: identity public keys (X25519 + Ed25519) + N one-time pre-key public keys with ordered integer IDs. The ceremony nonce from step 4 is included in the bundle.
8. Alice displays QR containing the key bundle.
9. Muttley scans QR with Bob.
10. Bob sends the key bundle (base64-encoded CBOR) to Dastardly's Bob via deep link transport. The Bob-to-Bob payload includes a 16-byte random nonce and UTC timestamp. The receiving Bob checks freshness (reject if older than `REPLAY_WINDOW`) and nonce uniqueness (reject if nonce already seen within the replay window) to prevent replay attacks.

### Recipient Side (Dastardly)

11. Dastardly's Bob receives the invitation. If pending count < 7, it is queued. Otherwise silently dropped.
12. Dastardly opens Bob, sees the pending invitation (Muttley's display name + phone number), and taps accept. This is an acceptance of the invitation only — verification is still required later.
13. Dastardly's Bob displays QR containing Muttley's key material.
14. Dastardly scans QR with his Alice.
15. Alice ingests Muttley as a contact with synthetic ID, persists identity public keys and pre-key bundle.
16. Alice generates her own key bundle: identity public keys (X25519 + Ed25519) + N one-time pre-key public keys with ordered integer IDs. The ceremony nonce from the original invitation is echoed back in the bundle.
17. Alice displays QR containing her key bundle.
18. Dastardly scans QR with his Bob.
19. Bob sends the response bundle to Muttley's Bob via deep link transport. Same replay protection as step 10 (16-byte random nonce + UTC timestamp, checked for freshness and uniqueness on receipt).

### Completion (Muttley)

20. Muttley's Bob receives Dastardly's key bundle.
21. Bob displays QR containing Dastardly's key material.
22. Muttley scans QR into his Alice.
23. Alice verifies the ceremony nonce in the received bundle exactly matches the nonce she stored for this contact at step 6 using constant-time equality (`MessageDigest.isEqual` or equivalent) as a defence-in-depth measure. Mismatch: hard delete + VACUUM and abort. Match: persists Dastardly's identity public keys and pre-key bundle.

Both Alices now hold each other's dual identity public keys (X25519 + Ed25519) and a batch of one-time pre-keys. Neither has touched a network. All private keys remain on their respective air-gapped devices. All contacts enter quarantine (`PENDING_VERIFICATION`) with a use-by date stamped at ceremony completion.

### Interruption at Any Step

Hard delete all ceremony state for that contact + VACUUM. Restart from step 1. No partial state survives.

### Total QR Scans

- Muttley: 3 scans (steps 5, 9, 22)
- Dastardly: 2 scans (steps 14, 18)
- 5 scan sessions total per engagement

## 4. Quarantine and Safety Number Verification

### Quarantine

All key material from the ceremony enters quarantine immediately. Contact state is set to `PENDING_VERIFICATION`. A `quarantineExpiry` timestamp is set at ceremony completion. Keys are persisted and encrypted but **functionally inert** — Alice refuses to encrypt or decrypt any messages for a contact in this state.

On every cold start, Alice checks all quarantined contacts. Any contact past its `quarantineExpiry` is hard deleted with all associated key material + VACUUM. Gone forever.

**Quarantine TTL:** Governed by `CEREMONY_TTL` — see Section 9, Parked Decisions.

### Safety Number Derivation

**Inputs:** Both X25519 identity public keys (see Conventions), concatenated in lexicographic order (lower key first).

**Algorithm:** SHA-256 iterated 5,197 times (prime). The identity keys are already 256-bit random, so the iteration count is a defence-in-depth measure, not a primary security property.

**Output:** 30 bytes, encoded as 12 groups. Each group consists of one NATO phonetic word + two decimal digits.

**Encoding per group:** 2.5 bytes (20 bits) per chunk. Top 5 bits modulo 26 map to a letter A-Z, which maps to the corresponding NATO phonetic word (ITU/ICAO International Radiotelephony Spelling Alphabet, per ITU Radio Regulations Appendix 14). Remaining 15 bits modulo 100 produce two decimal digits. 12 chunks consume all 30 bytes. Note: modulo 26 from 5 bits (0-31) gives letters A-F slightly higher probability (6.25% vs 3.125% for G-Z). This bias has no security impact on a human-verified safety number.

**Example output:** "Foxtrot 42, Lima 17, Tango 83, Alpha 09, Sierra 61, November 28, Kilo 44, Echo 73, Whiskey 15, Golf 90, Delta 56, Papa 31"

**Ordering rule:** Lexicographic comparison of X25519 identity public keys (lower first) is the single ordering rule used everywhere in the protocol — simultaneous initiation tiebreaker, safety number derivation, and any future operation requiring deterministic ordering. The Ed25519 signing key is never used for ordering.

### Verification Flow

1. Muttley and Dastardly contact each other over an independent trusted channel (voice or video call).
2. Both open Alice, navigate to quarantine contacts, select the other party.
3. Alice displays the safety number: 12 NATO word + digit groups, large, readable, segmented.
4. Both read their safety numbers aloud.
5. **Match:** Both tap **Verify**. Contact state moves to `VERIFIED`. `quarantineExpiry` set to null. `ceremonyNonce` set to null (no further purpose — dead data). Keys go live. Messaging enabled.
6. **Mismatch:** Both tap **Reject**. Hard delete contact + all key material + VACUUM. Entire engagement restarts from scratch.

### Partial Verification

If only one side verifies, the unverified Alice rejects all incoming messages for that contact. The channel is dead until the unverified side completes. Fails safe.

### Verification Screen

One screen. Contact display name. 12 NATO word + digit groups in large segmented blocks. Two actions: **Verify** and **Reject**. Nothing else. Zero cognitive overhead — the user is splitting attention between a phone call and this screen.

## 5. Key Bundle and Per-Message Cryptography

### Key Bundle Structure

The key bundle is carried as **QR Type 2 (Contact Card)**. It extends the existing Type 2 field registry with a ceremony nonce field. CBOR uses integer field keys only (string names are descriptive labels):

| Key | Name | Type | Size | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ikPubEd | `ByteArray` | 32 bytes | Sender's long-term Ed25519 identity public key (signature verification only) |
| 2 | ikPubX | `ByteArray` | 32 bytes | Sender's long-term X25519 identity public key (DH operations and protocol ordering) |
| 5 | opkList | `List<ByteArray>` | N x 32 bytes | One-time pre-key public keys, ordered by key ID |
| 7 | timestamp | `Long` | — | UTC Unix ms. Bundle creation time. |
| 8 | ceremonyNonce | `ByteArray` | 16 bytes | Ceremony correlation nonce from the initiating Bob (echoed through the full ceremony). New field — to be registered in `docs/design.md` Section 6. |

Each Alice maintains a long-term dual identity keypair: X25519 for DH operations and Ed25519 for signatures. Both public keys are exchanged during the initial ceremony. The Ed25519 key is used to verify bundle refresh signatures post-ceremony.

**Note on pre-key key IDs:** The existing Type 2 `opkList` is a flat `List<ByteArray>` where position = key ID (zero-indexed). This is sufficient — the sequential ordering is implicit in the list order. No separate key ID field is needed. Both sides track the consumption pointer by index.

**Pre-key count N:** To be determined by QR ECL POC results. Target range for POC: 40-80 pre-keys (balancing forward secrecy lifetime against QR scan reliability).

**QR error correction level for key bundles:** To be determined by QR ECL POC results.

### Sequential Key Consumption

- Keys are consumed in strict key ID order.
- Sender's Alice selects the next unconsumed key ID from the recipient's bundle.
- Key ID is included in the plaintext header so the recipient knows which key to use.
- Recipient Alice confirms the key ID matches the expected next. Match: decrypt and mark consumed.
- Zero remaining pre-keys: Alice cannot encrypt. UI displays "Key refresh required — no messages can be sent to [contact] until refresh completes."
- **Refresh threshold:** To be determined alongside pre-key count.

### Per-Message Encryption (Ephemeral DH)

1. Sender Alice generates a fresh ephemeral X25519 keypair.
2. DH: sender's ephemeral private key x recipient's one-time pre-key public key produces a shared secret.
3. HKDF-SHA256 derives a ChaCha20-Poly1305 session key from the shared secret. Salt: empty. Info string: `AliceBob_v1_MSG` (protocol-specific, prevents cross-protocol key reuse).
4. Encrypt message (CBOR-encoded plaintext) with ChaCha20-Poly1305 using derived key + random 12-byte nonce. The `keyId` and `ephemeralPublicKey` fields are bound as Additional Authenticated Data (AAD), preventing an attacker from swapping plaintext header fields without detection.
5. Ephemeral private key zeroed immediately after DH.
6. One-time pre-key marked consumed on both sides after use.

**Known limitation — no sender authentication in single DH.** The ephemeral DH does not mix in the sender's identity key. Any party who obtained a pre-key public key could theoretically craft valid ciphertext. In this threat model, pre-key private keys never leave Alice, so exploitation requires compromise of Alice herself — at which point the entire device is lost. This is a conscious deviation from Signal's X3DH (which uses four DH operations). Documented here as a known trade-off rather than an oversight.

### Message Payload Format

Standard QR protocol framing per `docs/design.md` Section 6:

```
[type: 1 byte][capabilities: 2 bytes][CBOR payload]
```

Type byte and capabilities are checked before CBOR parsing begins. There is no version byte — versioning by sequential number creates migration chains, which this system does not have.

The message payload uses **QR Type 3 (Message)** with its existing field registry:

| Key | Name | Type | Size | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | sequence | `Long` | — | Monotonic sender-recipient sequence number (maps to pre-key consumption index) |
| 2 | timestamp | `Long` | — | UTC Unix ms. Display only. Never used for ordering. |
| 3 | nonce | `ByteArray` | 12 bytes | ChaCha20-Poly1305 nonce |
| 4 | header | `ByteArray` | 40 bytes | dhsPub (32 bytes, ephemeral DH public key) ∥ PN (4 bytes) ∥ Ns (4 bytes) |
| 5 | ciphertext | `ByteArray` | variable | AEAD output: ciphertext + 16-byte Poly1305 tag |

The `sequence` field serves as the pre-key index (plaintext, recipient uses it to select the decryption key). The `header` field carries the ephemeral DH public key, which is bound as AAD alongside the `sequence` field to prevent an attacker from swapping plaintext header fields without detection.

## 6. Post-Ceremony Message Handling

### Out-of-Order Messages

If Alice receives a message with key ID N+X but expects key ID N:

1. Alice persists the message as ciphertext only in Room (buffered messages table).
2. Alice does not decrypt or surface it.
3. When the missing messages arrive and the full chain resolves, Alice decrypts and displays them one at a time in sequence order.
4. Each message gets its own read cycle and two-step destruction.
5. UI notification while waiting: "You have X messages pending — awaiting earlier messages in sequence."

### Read-Once Semantics

1. Alice decrypts and displays one message. Only one decrypted plaintext in memory at any moment.
2. User reads the message.
3. User taps "Done" (deliberate intent signal).
4. Confirmation dialog: "Delete this message permanently?"
5. User confirms. Row hard-deleted from Room.
6. Next message in sequence appears (if any).
7. VACUUM deferred until read session ends, next cold start, or next app resume.

No conversation history is persisted for properly sequenced messages. Messages exist in memory during display and vanish on acknowledgement.

### Stale Message Expiry

Buffered out-of-order messages receive a use-by date stamped at arrival time.

- On every cold start, Alice checks all buffered messages.
- Any message past its use-by date: hard delete + VACUUM.
- Associated key IDs are burned. Alice advances her expected-next pointer past the gap.
- The conversation has a permanent, visible gap. Both parties are aware.
- **Stale message TTL:** Governed by `CEREMONY_TTL` — see Section 9, Parked Decisions.

### VACUUM Policy

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Cold start | VACUUM |
| App resume (background to foreground) | VACUUM |
| Quarantine/stale expiry deletion (during cold start check) | Part of cold start VACUUM |
| During read sessions | Hard delete per message, VACUUM deferred |

## 7. Bundle Refresh

When the count of remaining unconsumed one-time pre-keys for a contact drops below the refresh threshold:

### Refresh Bundle Structure

The refresh bundle uses **QR Type 4 (Key Rotation)** — a separate CBOR structure from the initial key bundle. It does not contain identity keys or a ceremony nonce — those are already established and verified. Integer field keys per protocol rules:

| Key | Name | Type | Size | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | opkList | `List<ByteArray>` | N x 32 bytes | New one-time pre-key public keys, sequential IDs continuing from the last generated |
| 4 | signature | `ByteArray` | 64 bytes | Ed25519 signature over the CBOR-encoded `opkList` field |
| 6 | timestamp | `Long` | — | UTC Unix ms. Refresh creation time. |

Field keys 4 and 5 reuse the existing Type 4 numbering convention (4 = signature, 5 = new key material). Field key 6 = timestamp, consistent with existing types.

### Refresh Flow

1. Alice generates a new batch of N one-time pre-keys with IDs continuing sequentially from the last generated ID.
2. Alice signs the refresh bundle with her Ed25519 identity private key.
3. Alice displays QR containing the signed refresh bundle.
4. User scans with Bob.
5. Bob sends the signed refresh bundle to the contact's Bob via deep link transport.
6. Contact's Bob displays QR for their Alice to ingest.
7. Contact's Alice verifies the Ed25519 signature against the already-verified signing public key.
8. Alice rejects any refresh bundle containing key IDs that overlap with already-held key IDs. This prevents replay of a previously valid signed refresh bundle.
9. Signature valid and key IDs are fresh: new pre-keys persisted and available for use.
10. Signature invalid or duplicate key IDs: refresh rejected, hard delete + VACUUM. Contact notified out-of-band.

### Differences from Initial Engagement

| Aspect | Initial Engagement | Bundle Refresh |
|---|---|---|
| Identity key exchange | Yes | No — already established |
| Quarantine | Yes | No — identity already verified |
| Safety number verification | Yes — voice/video call | No — Ed25519 signature replaces it |
| QR scans per side | 3 initiator, 2 recipient | 2 (Alice to Bob, Bob to Alice on receiving end) |
| Failure response | Hard delete + restart ceremony | Hard delete refresh + investigate out-of-band |

### Refresh Trigger UX

Alice displays a persistent indicator when pre-keys are running low: "Key refresh needed for [contact]." The user initiates the refresh at their convenience. Background/automatic refresh is impossible — QR transport requires human action.

## 8. Alice's Persistence Model

All columns encrypted via Room TypeConverters. Synthetic auto-generated primary keys throughout. ChaCha20-Poly1305 encryption with 12-byte nonce prepended to ciphertext.

### Tables

| Table | Contents | Expiry |
|---|---|---|
| **Own identity** | Alice's long-term dual identity keypair (X25519 + Ed25519) | Never. This IS Alice. |
| **Contacts** | Synthetic ID, phone number, display name, X25519 identity public key, Ed25519 signing public key, ceremony nonce, verification state (`PENDING_VERIFICATION` / `VERIFIED`), `quarantineExpiry` (nullable) | Quarantined contacts expire on use-by date |
| **Own pre-keys** | Generated one-time pre-key private halves with key IDs and consumed flag, per contact | Never. Consumed flag handles lifecycle. |
| **Received pre-keys** | Received one-time pre-key public halves with key IDs and consumed flag, per contact | Never. Consumed flag handles lifecycle. |
| **Buffered messages** | Ciphertext of out-of-order messages with sender, key ID, arrival timestamp, expiry timestamp | Expire on use-by date |

**Engagement state table: eliminated.** Restart-from-zero means no ceremony state survives interruption.

**Targeted expiry:** Use-by dates apply only to quarantined contacts and buffered messages. All other entities persist until explicitly destroyed.

### Deletion Semantics

Every deletion in Alice is a hard delete (`DELETE FROM`) followed by VACUUM at the next appropriate moment (cold start, app resume, or end of read session). No soft deletes. No tombstones. No marked-for-deletion flags.

### New Device

New device = new Alice = new identity keypair. All contacts on the other side must hard delete the old contact and re-engage from scratch. Full ceremony + full verification. No migration path within the engagement system. Device Migration (QR type 7) is a separate concern.

## 9. Parked Decisions

### QR ECL POC Dependent

These decisions are blocked on empirical testing of QR error correction levels on real Pixel hardware:

| Decision | Dependency |
|---|---|
| Pre-key count N per bundle | Maximum CBOR payload size at chosen ECL. Target range: 40-80. |
| QR error correction level for key bundle QR | POC results across all ECL levels on Pixel devices |
| Bundle refresh threshold | Determined as percentage of N once N is known. Starting point: `floor(N / 3)`. |

A dedicated POC will test all error correction levels to determine the actual limits on real hardware before these values are set.

### Independent

These decisions are not blocked on any POC but remain open:

| Decision | Notes |
|---|---|
| `CEREMONY_TTL` | **Locked: 13 days (prime).** A single constant governing both quarantine expiry and stale message expiry. No security or UX reason for them to diverge. Long enough to schedule a verification call, short enough to limit damage if one side is compromised. |
| `REPLAY_WINDOW` | **Locked: 11 minutes (prime).** Freshness window for Bob-to-Bob payload timestamps. Bob retains seen nonces for this duration only. If Bob's app data is cleared, the replay cache is lost — a replayed payload within the freshness window would be accepted. Acceptable: the paranoid posture means any interrupted or suspect ceremony triggers hard delete anyway. |

## 10. Out of Scope

The following are explicitly excluded from this specification and require their own design sessions:

- **QR2 attestation** — the dual-QR app integrity verification system
- **Device Migration** — QR type 7, transferring Alice state to a new device
- **Group messaging** — QR type 9, deferred to V2
- **Safety number QR verification** — not applicable (users never meet in person)

## 11. Known Limitations

Documented trade-offs, not oversights:

| Limitation | Rationale | Section |
|---|---|---|
| No sender authentication in single DH | Pre-key private keys never leave Alice. Exploitation requires full device compromise. Conscious deviation from X3DH's four-DH model. | 5 |
| Silent invitation drop at cap | Bob-to-Bob channel is untrusted — delivery receipts would be unreliable. Users confirm receipt out-of-band. | 2 |
| Safety number letter bias (A-F overrepresented) | Modulo 26 from 5 bits. No security impact on human-verified comparison. | 4 |
| Replay cache lost on Bob app data clear | Nonce replay cache is in-memory/local only. Cleared cache within `REPLAY_WINDOW` allows replay. Acceptable given restart-from-zero posture. | 3 |
